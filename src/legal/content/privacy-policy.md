# Privacy Policy

## 1. Who controls your personal data

GEN H HOLDINGS SDN. BHD. (Company No. 202601020381 (1682478-D)), trading as Verae Health, is the data controller for the personal data it determines how and why to process. The independent doctor and Innoquest Pathology Sdn Bhd may also be separate data controllers for records and activities under their professional or legal responsibility. The parties coordinate access, security, retention and continuity of care.

For questions or requests, contact **privacy@veraehealth.com** or write to Verae at Level 7, Mercu 3, No. 3, Jalan Bangsar, KL Eco City, 59200 Kuala Lumpur, W.P. Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia.

## 2. Personal data we collect

We may collect:

- identity and eligibility data, such as name, identity-document details, date of birth, sex, photograph, location and verification status;
- contact, account and security data, such as email, phone, address, login records, device/browser data and authentication events;
- health and medical data, including symptoms, history, family history, medicines, allergies, lifestyle, questionnaire answers, consultation notes, uploaded documents, referrals and care communications;
- laboratory and specimen data, including orders, collection details, biomarkers, results, flags and reports;
- service and transaction data, such as membership, appointments, attendance, payments, receipts, cancellations and support interactions;
- communications and feedback, including secure messages, complaints, survey responses and consent choices; and
- technical and audit data needed to operate, secure and improve the service, such as timestamps, access logs, error records and report versions.

We do not routinely record consultation audio or video. If recording is proposed, you will receive a separate explanation and consent request before it begins.

## 3. Sources of personal data

We collect data from you; your authorised representative; your Verae doctor; partner laboratories and collection providers; healthcare providers you ask to share records; payment, identity-verification and technical service providers; and automatically from your use of the portal. If you provide another person’s information, such as an emergency contact, you confirm that you are authorised to do so and have told them how Verae will use it.

## 4. Why we process personal data

We process personal data as necessary and lawful to:

- create and secure your account and verify identity, age, location and suitability;
- arrange and deliver teleconsultations, testing, physician review, reports, follow-up and continuity of care;
- communicate service, preparation, safety and critical-result information;
- process payments, refunds, membership administration and support;
- create and retain clinical, consent and audit records;
- protect members, staff and systems; prevent fraud and misuse; investigate incidents; and maintain backups and service continuity;
- manage complaints, quality assurance, clinical governance, professional review and service improvement using the minimum necessary data;
- comply with legal, regulatory, professional, insurance, accounting and reporting obligations and respond to lawful requests; and
- send marketing only where you have made a separate choice or where otherwise permitted by law, with an easy opt-out.

Health information is sensitive personal data. Where Malaysian law requires express consent, we rely on the Informed Consent Policy. Some processing may also be required to provide care, protect vital interests, establish or defend legal rights, or comply with law and professional duties. Verae will not rely on a withdrawal of consent to erase or conceal a record that it is legally or clinically required to keep.

## 5. Data required from you

Data marked as required is needed to assess suitability, safely provide the service, process payment or meet record-keeping obligations. If you do not provide it, Verae or your doctor may be unable to proceed, may limit the service, or may recommend in-person care. Optional fields will be identified where practicable.

## 6. Who receives personal data

We disclose only data reasonably necessary for the stated purpose to:

- your Verae doctor and authorised members of the clinical team;
- partner laboratories, phlebotomy/collection providers and referral providers;
- Verae personnel who need access for clinical operations, member support, privacy, security, quality, legal or finance functions;
- contracted processors providing hosting, secure communications, video, identity verification, payment, analytics, customer support, backup, cybersecurity and professional services;
- another healthcare provider or person at your request or with your consent;
- insurers, professional advisers, regulators, law-enforcement bodies, courts or other parties where disclosure is required or permitted by law; and
- an emergency contact, healthcare provider or authority where a limited disclosure is reasonably necessary to protect life or health and is permitted by law and professional duties.

We do not sell personal data. We require processors to protect data and use it only for contracted purposes. Key providers include Innoquest Pathology Sdn Bhd for laboratory services, Remedi Innovations Sdn Bhd for electronic health-record storage and processing, and Google Workspace/Google Meet for email and video consultation. A current category-level list of providers is available from Verae on request.

## 7. Technology, analytics and automated processing

Verae does not currently use AI, automated clinical decision-making, profiling or automated interpretation software. Verae does not use identifiable member health information to train AI models. If that changes, Verae will update this Policy and obtain any consent or other lawful basis required before the new use.

We may use de-identified or aggregated information for service statistics, quality and planning only where individuals are not reasonably identifiable. Research, publication or product development unrelated to your care requires separate governance and, where required, separate express consent. Refusal will not affect ordinary care.

## 8. Location and cross-border transfers

Verae hosts and processes member records—including portal data, questionnaire answers, laboratory results, consultation notes, reports, care plans and consent records—in Malaysia.

Verae uses Google Workspace and Google Meet for email and video consultations. The live audio/video stream and limited technical data required to operate a consultation may be processed by Google on global infrastructure. Verae does not routinely record or store consultation audio/video. Google Meet encrypts meeting data in transit and at rest; Verae restricts access to authorised users and applies available enterprise security, administrator controls, audit logging and retention settings. Google is used as a service provider under its applicable data-processing terms. Verae does not describe Google processing as Malaysia-hosted and keeps the Google Workspace/Meet configuration under security and privacy review.

Except for this limited video-service processing, Verae does not intentionally transfer member records outside Malaysia. Any additional cross-border transfer will be assessed and documented, use appropriate contractual and security safeguards, comply with section 129 of the PDPA, and be notified or consented to where required by law.

## 9. How we protect personal data

Verae uses proportionate administrative, physical and technical safeguards, including role-based access, authentication, encryption in transit and at rest, access logging, secure backups, vendor controls, staff confidentiality obligations, incident response and periodic security review. No system is completely risk-free. If a data breach creates the applicable level of risk, Verae will notify the Personal Data Protection Commissioner and affected individuals as required by law.

## 10. Retention and deletion

We keep each category of data only as long as necessary for care, continuity, safety, legal claims, audit, professional and regulatory obligations, tax/accounting requirements and approved business purposes. We then securely delete or de-identify it.

Verae’s retention schedule is:

- clinical records, laboratory results, reports, care plans, prescriptions and consent records: at least seven years after the last clinical entry or service interaction, and longer where required by law, professional guidance, a complaint, investigation, dispute, legal hold or continuity-of-care need;
- financial and tax records: seven years after the relevant transaction or financial year, or longer where required by law;
- support, complaint and safety records: seven years where they relate to clinical care, safety or a complaint, otherwise three years after closure;
- account and access-security logs: 24 months after the relevant event, unless needed for an investigation or legal hold;
- marketing consent and suppression records: for as long as needed to honour the member’s choice and demonstrate compliance; and
- backup copies: deleted or overwritten through the applicable backup cycle, ordinarily within 90 days after primary deletion, subject to security and legal holds.

These periods may be extended where required by law, professional guidance, a complaint, investigation, dispute, legal hold or continuity-of-care need. Verae will not delete records that must be retained for clinical, legal, safety, regulatory or dispute-related reasons.

Withdrawal of consent or account closure normally stops future optional processing but does not automatically delete the clinical record or data that must be retained. Data in secure backups may remain until the backup cycle expires, with access restricted.

## 11. Your rights and choices

Subject to the PDPA and lawful exceptions, you may:

- ask whether Verae processes your personal data and request access;
- request correction of inaccurate, incomplete, misleading or outdated data;
- withdraw consent for future processing;
- object to or prevent processing likely to cause damage or distress in circumstances recognised by law;
- opt out of direct marketing at any time;
- ask questions about technology-assisted processing and request human review where applicable; and
- make a complaint to Verae or the Personal Data Protection Commissioner.

Send a request to **privacy@veraehealth.com**. Verae may verify your identity and may charge only a fee permitted by law. Verae will explain any lawful refusal or limitation. Withdrawal may mean the service cannot continue safely, but it will not affect processing already lawfully completed.

## 12. Cookies and portal analytics

The portal uses strictly necessary technologies for login, security, preferences and service operation. Verae does not use advertising pixels, session-recording tools or third-party behavioural analytics on authenticated health pages.

## 13. Changes and language

Verae may update this Policy to reflect service, provider or legal changes. Material changes will be highlighted and, where required, notified before they take effect or presented for fresh consent. The effective date and previous accepted version will be retained.
